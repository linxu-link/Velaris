What Data We Process

Atmosphere Clock is a landscape immersive scene app for browsing and editing local scenes, backgrounds, videos, ambient sounds, brightness, timers, and particle effects. Atmosphere Clock does not actively collect, upload, or sell users' personal information. Most data processing in the app happens locally on the user's device.

Local Media

When a user selects local images, videos, or audio files as scene assets, the app may request access to the device media library:

  • Images and videos: used to choose scene backgrounds or video assets.
  • Audio: used to choose local ambient sound assets.

These media files are used only for local features explicitly chosen by the user. Atmosphere Clock does not upload user-selected local media to developer servers.

Advertising and Consent Management

Atmosphere Clock integrates Google advertising services. The Google Ads SDK may process device identifiers, advertising IDs, ad interactions, approximate location, diagnostic information, and related data according to Google's own policies for ad delivery, frequency capping, anti-fraud, analytics, and measurement.

If advertising consent management is required in the user's region, Atmosphere Clock uses Google's consent management capabilities to present ad privacy options. Users can manage ad privacy choices through system settings or relevant in-app entry points.

Data Sharing

Atmosphere Clock does not share users' local media files with developer servers.

Because the app integrates Google advertising services, Google may process advertising-related data according to its SDK and service policies. For details, please refer to the Google Privacy Policy.

Data Retention and Deletion

Atmosphere Clock stores locally the scene configurations created or edited by users, such as titles, descriptions, asset references, sound settings, brightness, timers, and particle effects. Users can delete or modify this data through in-app features.

Users can also clear app data in Android system settings or uninstall the app to remove locally stored data.

Permission Management

Users can revoke media access permissions in Android system settings. After permissions are revoked, features that depend on the local media library may no longer be able to read the corresponding assets, but built-in scenes and built-in sounds can still be used.

Children's Privacy

Atmosphere Clock is not specifically directed to children. The developer does not actively collect personal information from children.

Policy Updates

If app features, permissions, or third-party SDKs change, this privacy policy may be updated. The updated policy will be provided through an in-app entry point or a public web page.
